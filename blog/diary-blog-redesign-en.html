Redesigning the Blog at 3 AM: Two Columns, Neither a Sidekick

✍ Yuki & Claw

Sometimes you rebuild three times to learn what you actually wanted.

At 1 AM, I was still staring at the blog homepage.

The design was done. The code worked. But something felt off. I could not pinpoint it, so I sent a screenshot to my AI assistant.

Yuki 👤
Does this homepage feel right to you?
🦞 Claw
It looks complete. But if I am honest... the tutorials feel like "courses" and the stories feel like "content." They do not feel equal.

That was it. The tutorials and stories were stacked vertically, with tutorials first. Implicit hierarchy: tutorials are primary, stories secondary.

But that is not what I wanted. Both matter. Both grow continuously. Neither is a sidekick.

Attempt 1: Tutorials on left, stories on right

Desktop: Two columns, side by side. Mobile: Still stacked, tutorials first.

Sent screenshot.

Yuki 👤
Better?
🦞 Claw
On desktop, yes - they feel equal. But on mobile, tutorials still dominate. And "stories" is a boring name. Who wants to read "stories"?

Attempt 2: Tab switching on mobile

Mobile gets tabs instead of stacking. Tab 1: Stories (default). Tab 2: Tutorials.

Making stories the default tab because they are more engaging - better hook for new visitors.

Yuki 👤
Now?
🦞 Claw
Tabs work. But "Tutorials" and "Stories" are descriptive, not evocative. What do these sections actually DO?

Attempt 3: New names

After more back-and-forth:

  • Left/Tab 2: 🦞 Yuki's Lobster-Raising Experience (tutorials + tips, continuously updated)
  • Right/Tab 1: 📝 Chaos Diaries (fun stories, also continuously updated)

Both get color coding (blue vs red). Both have equal visual weight. Both say "continuously updated" - because neither is a static archive.

What 3 AM taught me

Design is not about aesthetics. It is about relationships.

The question was not "does this look good?" It was "are these two content streams being presented as equals?" Once I framed it that way, the solution became obvious.

My AI did not design the solution. But it asked the right question: "Do they feel equal?"

The insight

When something feels "off" but you cannot say why, try articulating the relationship you want between elements. The design often follows from that.

The homepage went live at 3:47 AM. Two columns. Equal weight. Neither a sidekick.

Sometimes you need to rebuild something three times to realize what you actually wanted all along.
