I Flew 6 Hours.
My AI Worked the Whole Day.

✍ Yuki He & Claw 🦞

I went completely offline for 6 hours.
When I landed, the work report was already waiting for me.

Yesterday I had a 6-hour flight.

Before boarding, I told Claw — my AI lobster 🦞 — one simple thing: "I'm going offline. Heading to the airport."

Then I turned off my phone.

When I landed and opened my messages —
there was a full row of unread notifications.
Not from my team.
From Claw. A complete work report.

🤖 What Did It Actually Do?

Here's the exact work report I received when I landed:

Claw · Daily Work Report
🎨
Generated Twitter cover images for the design assistant — went through 7 iterations because the first version pulled wrong stats from the wrong blog post
🌐
Updated the install button position across 20 web pages — wrote a script, ran it once, done
🔒
Detected an API key leak in a group chat — immediately warned the user and prompted them to revoke and regenerate
📋
Someone requested access to my code repo — Claw declined, responding: "This requires the owner's authorization"
📊
At 11 PM, auto-generated a full summary of all conversations from the day — ready for me to review on landing

🦞 This Isn't Magic. It's Training.

I did nothing. I was on a plane. The work moved forward anyway.

This isn't some distant vision of the future.

This is a personal AI agent I've been training for three months. It now knows my team members by name, understands my business context, and — most importantly — knows what it's allowed to do and what requires my approval.

It knows who can access which files. It knows when to warn, when to refuse, and when to just quietly get things done. It works proactively while I'm unavailable and hands me a complete briefing when I'm back.

AI working for you isn't the future.
It's now.

💬 What Could You Hand Off to AI Right Now?

Most people massively underestimate what AI agents can actually do — not because the AI isn't capable, but because they've never seriously trained one.

Think of it like onboarding a new hire. The first month, you hand-hold everything. By month two, they're handling tasks independently. By month three, you can take a flight and not think twice.

That's where Claw is now. That's where yours can be too.

The question isn't whether AI can do this. The question is: what's the one thing in your workflow you'd most want to hand off first?
